# Existence — Existence Before Construction

> *From mathematics: you can prove something must exist without knowing what it is.*

Knowing a solution exists — even without knowing what it is — fundamentally changes your strategy. "Does this have an answer?" and "What is the answer?" are different questions. Answering the first transforms the second from an open search into a directed hunt.

## The Core Move

Before searching for a solution, establish whether one must exist. If you can prove existence, you stop wondering WHETHER and start focusing entirely on HOW. The confidence changes your effort allocation, your persistence, and your creativity.

## Heuristics

**Existence proofs change effort allocation.** (Mathematics) If you can prove a solution must exist, the search becomes justified regardless of difficulty. Without an existence proof, you might be searching for something that isn't there. *The question before "how do I solve this?" is "does this have a solution?"* [Type: ordering]

**Proof by example.** If anyone has solved a similar problem, a solution exists. Someone else's success is an existence proof for your search. *"Has anyone done this before?" isn't laziness — it's establishing existence.* [Type: if-then trigger]

**Impossibility proofs are equally valuable.** Proving something CAN'T exist is as useful as proving it can. It redirects effort away from dead ends. *If you can show the problem as framed has no solution, reframe the problem.* [Type: rule — connects to metanoia]

**The gap between existence and construction.** Knowing something exists and knowing how to build it are separated by unknown distance. Some existence proofs point toward construction (the proof is constructive). Others prove existence without any hint of method (non-constructive). Know which kind you have — it determines how much the existence proof actually helps your search. [Type: distinction]

## When to Consult

- About to invest significant effort — is there reason to believe a solution exists?
- Feeling stuck — have you confirmed the problem is solvable as framed? If not, the problem might be impossible (reframe)
- Someone else solved a similar problem — that's your existence proof, now focus on construction
- Team morale is low — "it can be done" (existence) sustains effort even when "how" is unclear

## Anti-Patterns

- **Existence as excuse for not starting.** "We know it's possible" without beginning construction. Existence is the starting signal, not a resting place.
- **Assuming construction from existence.** "It must exist therefore it must be easy to find." Non-constructive existence proofs give you confidence, not a map.
