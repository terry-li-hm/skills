# Ubuntu — Cognition Is Relational

> *Ubuntu: Zulu/Xhosa — "I am because we are." Umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu.*

Every other mined skill assumes an individual thinker making moves. Ubuntu says: the individual thinker is an impoverished unit. Thinking done in relationship is qualitatively different — not just better-informed, but structurally different. The relationship generates thoughts that neither party contains alone.

## The Core Move

Before thinking alone, think with someone. Not to get their opinion — to activate the relational mode of cognition that solitary thinking can't access.

## Heuristics

**I am because we are.** (Ubuntu philosophy) Identity is relational, not individual. You don't exist as an isolated self — you exist through connections. Decisions made in isolation are impoverished not because you lack information, but because thinking IS relational. [Type: reframe]

**Knowledge is performance.** (Griot tradition) The griot carries knowledge in memory and enacts it — each retelling adapts to audience and moment. Knowledge isn't stored then retrieved; it's alive in the person and the relationship. A good consultant outperforms a good report because the knowledge is alive, not frozen. [Type: distinction — alive knowledge vs. stored knowledge]

**Consensus through dialogue (indaba).** Decisions through extended communal dialogue where everyone speaks. Not voting — talking until alignment emerges. The process IS the decision. Slow consensus builds commitment that fast decisions don't. [Type: spectrum — speed of decision vs. depth of buy-in]

**Proverbs as compressed transmission.** "The axe forgets; the tree remembers." The best heuristic doesn't explain itself — it gives you enough to recognise the pattern when you encounter it. Over-explained heuristics are less memorable and less useful than evocative ones. [Type: rule for how to write heuristics]

## When to Consult

- Making a decision in isolation — have you thought WITH someone, not just consulted them?
- A problem feels stuck — solitary thinking may have exhausted what it can produce
- Designing systems for individual users — is the unit of value actually the relationship?
- Consilium feels mechanical — the structure is there but the relational quality isn't
- Writing heuristics — is this evocative enough, or over-explained?

## Anti-Patterns

- **Performative collaboration.** Meeting ≠ thinking together. Most meetings are serial monologues, not relational cognition.
- **Using ubuntu to avoid responsibility.** "We decided together" can diffuse accountability. Ubuntu is about thinking together, not hiding behind consensus.
