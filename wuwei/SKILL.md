# Wu Wei — Working With Momentum

> *Wu wei (無為): Chinese — non-action, or more precisely, action that doesn't force.*

The Western cognitive toolkit defaults to active engagement: hold problems (Einstein), test at extremes (reductio), build systems for disconfirmation (Darwin). Wu wei is the complementary move — read where the energy already wants to go, and work with it.

## The Core Move

Stop pushing against the system. Read its momentum. Align your action with the existing flow. The most effective intervention often works with the grain, not against it.

## Heuristics

**Non-action as action.** (Laozi) Water doesn't fight the rock — it goes around. When you're pushing hard and meeting resistance, the resistance is information. The system is telling you the direction it wants to move. Try working with that direction instead of against it. [Type: reframe]

**Rhythm and timing.** (Musashi — hyōshi) Every situation has a rhythm. Victory comes from perceiving and syncing with the natural tempo, or disrupting the opponent's rhythm. You can't force timing — you read it. Projects, negotiations, career moves all have rhythms. Forcing action against the rhythm wastes energy. [Type: signal — when effort feels disproportionate to results, you may be fighting the rhythm]

**Strategic momentum (势 shì).** (Sun Tzu) Not position — potential energy. A boulder at the top of a hill has shì before it moves. Arrange conditions where the desired outcome becomes natural, then barely push. Don't fight the decisive battle — arrange the situation so it decides itself. [Type: rule]

**Winning without fighting (不战而胜).** (Sun Tzu) The highest strategy makes conflict unnecessary. The best solution dissolves the problem rather than solving it. Before attacking a problem head-on, ask: can the conditions be changed so the problem ceases to exist? [Type: if-then trigger]

## When to Consult

- Meeting persistent resistance — you're pushing hard but nothing moves
- Timing feels wrong — the action is right but the moment isn't
- Effort is disproportionate to results — you're working hard for little return
- Before forcing a decision — is there a way the situation resolves itself?
- After reductio or diagnosis stalls — you've tested the extremes, now read the flow

## Anti-Patterns

- **Using wu wei as excuse for passivity.** Wu wei is active — reading, positioning, timing. Not doing nothing.
- **Ignoring genuine urgency.** Some situations need force. Wu wei is a tool, not a philosophy of life.
- **Romanticising "flow."** The test: does working with the grain produce better outcomes? If forcing produces better outcomes, force.
