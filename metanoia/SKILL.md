# Metanoia — Representation Shifting

> *Metanoia: Greek meta + nous — a shift in how the mind sees.*

When you're stuck, the default is to try harder within the same frame. Metanoia says: change the frame first. The problem may be trivial in a different representation.

## The Core Move

The same problem looks different depending on how you represent it. Shifting representation isn't a creativity trick — it's a cognitive tool with a strong empirical track record across domains.

## Heuristics

**Switch sensory mode.** (Einstein) He thought in images and physical sensations, not words. "What would it feel like to ride a beam of light?" is a sensory question. When stuck on an abstract problem, convert it to a physical or visual one. [Type: if-then trigger]

**Switch notation.** (Feynman) Feynman diagrams weren't illustration — they were a new way to see the same math. If a problem is hard in one notation, it may be trivial in another. The representation is doing more work than you think. [Type: reframe]

**Switch discipline.** (Munger) The latticework of mental models. No single discipline is sufficient. The person with one model forces every problem to fit it. When a problem resists your field's tools, borrow tools from an adjacent field. [Type: rule]

**Switch timescale.** (Stoicism — view from above) Marcus Aurelius zooms out — see your problem from the scale of the week, the year, the decade, the century. Most urgencies shrink. Most important things grow. Not nihilism — proportion. [Type: default with override — default to current timescale, but when stuck or anxious, zoom out]

**Switch by analogy.** (Darwin) Artificial selection → natural selection. The analogy wasn't illustration — it was the reasoning path. If you can't find an analogy for your idea in an established domain, you may not understand it yet. [Type: signal]

## When to Consult

- Diagnosis has stalled — you've tried multiple solutions but none feel right
- A problem feels "hard" — it might be hard in this representation, easy in another
- You're going in circles — same frame, different attempts
- Architecture feels clunky — the feeling of wrongness is a representation signal

## Anti-Patterns

- **Switching for novelty, not insight.** The test: does the new representation reveal something the old one hid? If not, you just redecorated.
- **Collecting representations without committing.** Metanoia is a tool for getting unstuck, not an excuse to avoid deciding.
