# Prescience — The Pathology of Excessive Analysis

> *From Dune: Paul Atreides can see the future — and it paralyses him.*

The entire skill network is biased toward "look more, analyse deeper, test at extremes." Aporia says map your ignorance. Reductio says test at the limits. Adversaria says see from every angle. Prescience says: that has a failure mode. Past an optimal point, more analysis DECREASES your ability to act.

## The Core Move

Recognise when analysis is no longer serving action. Every additional scenario you consider, every failure mode you identify, every angle you examine — each one has a cost: decision paralysis. There's an optimal depth of analysis, and going past it is as harmful as stopping too early.

## Heuristics

**The prescience trap.** (Dune — Paul Atreides) Seeing all possible outcomes makes choosing impossible because you see the cost of every path. More information about consequences can decrease rather than increase your ability to act. [Type: anti-pattern]

**The Golden Path requires blindness.** (Dune — Leto II) The only way forward was to make the future unpredictable again. Sometimes you must deliberately limit your analysis to preserve the ability to act. Choosing NOT to look is itself a strategic move. [Type: reframe]

**Optimal analysis depth exists.** Too little → reckless. Too much → paralysed. The optimum depends on reversibility: reversible decisions warrant less analysis, irreversible decisions warrant more. But even irreversible decisions have a point of diminishing returns. [Type: spectrum]

**The Gom Jabbar inversion.** The Gom Jabbar tests whether you can endure pain without flinching. Prescience is the opposite test: can you endure uncertainty without analysing? The animal analyses until it's safe. The human acts while it's still uncertain. [Type: reframe]

## When to Consult

- Every option looks flawed — you may have analysed past the useful point
- You keep finding MORE failure modes instead of converging — analysis is diverging, not converging
- The team is stuck in planning — "one more review" is the prescience trap
- You feel unable to choose — that feeling IS the signal
- After using reductio, aporia, adversaria — check: did the analysis help or add paralysis?

## Anti-Patterns

- **Using prescience to justify laziness.** "Analysis paralysis" is real, but so is "acted without thinking." Prescience is for people who've already done the analysis, not for people avoiding it.
- **Fixed analysis budgets.** "Always decide in 5 minutes" is as wrong as "always analyse exhaustively." The optimum shifts with stakes and reversibility.
