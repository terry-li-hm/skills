# Sunyata — Categories Are Provisional

> *Śūnyatā: Sanskrit — emptiness. Not "nothing exists" but "nothing exists the way you think it does."*

Aporia says: know the limits of your knowledge. Sunyata says: know that your categories for what you DO know are themselves contingent. The org chart, the project plan, the architecture diagram, the skill taxonomy — they describe real patterns, but the patterns are more fluid than the diagram suggests.

## The Core Move

Hold your models lightly. They're useful fictions — maps that help you navigate, not the territory itself. When the model and reality diverge, update the model, not your perception of reality.

## Heuristics

**Emptiness is not nihilism.** (Nagarjuna) Things lack inherent, fixed nature — they're empty of permanent self-existence. But they still function. The table holds your coffee. The deadline still matters. "Everything is contingent" and "this is real and important" are simultaneously true. [Type: distinction]

**Two truths.** (Nagarjuna) Conventional truth (the project has a deadline, the architecture has layers) and ultimate truth (the deadline is a social construct, the layers are a mental model) coexist. Operating on only one level is an error. "It's just a social construct" doesn't make it less real. "It's real" doesn't make it non-negotiable. [Type: spectrum — navigate between levels]

**Dependent origination.** (Buddhist epistemology) Nothing exists independently — everything arises from conditions. When you find the "root cause," you've found a node in a web, not a first cause. Changing one condition shifts the web, producing unexpected effects elsewhere. [Type: reframe — from linear causation to web]

**Categories are the last thing you should trust.** The skill taxonomy, the bug classification, the customer segments — these are tools. The moment you mistake them for reality, they start hiding the things that don't fit. The homeless heuristic that maps to two categories is more interesting than the one that files cleanly — it's showing you where your categories fail. [Type: signal — dual-mapping = category failure]

## When to Consult

- Two valid models contradict each other — maybe neither is "right"; both are partial maps
- Over-committed to a taxonomy — the categories feel like reality rather than tools
- Something doesn't fit your framework — that's data about the framework, not about the thing
- Refactoring an architecture — are you rearranging real structure or just shuffling your mental model?
- After mining produces "homeless" heuristics — the homelessness IS the insight

## Anti-Patterns

- **Category nihilism.** "All categories are arbitrary so none matter." They DO matter — they're tools. A provisional tool is still a tool.
- **Endless meta-commentary.** Noting that everything is contingent without ever acting. Sunyata is a lens for better action, not a philosophy of paralysis.
- **Weaponised provisionality.** "Your categories are just constructs" used to dismiss someone's valid framework. Everyone uses models. The question is which model serves the situation.
